Privacy Policy

Border AI helps people understand Canadian visa pathways, organize application materials, and connect with independent advisors. This policy explains what information we collect, how we use it, and the choices you have.

1. Scope

This Privacy Policy applies to the Border AI website, eligibility check, account pages, dashboard, document tools, advisor marketplace, communications, and related services that link to this policy. By using Border AI, you agree that we may process information as described here.

Border AI is not affiliated with Immigration, Refugees and Citizenship Canada, any embassy, consulate, or government agency. Border AI is not a law firm and does not provide legal advice.

2. Information We Collect

  • Account and contact information, such as your name, email address, login details, and communication preferences.
  • Eligibility and profile information, such as citizenship, destination, travel purpose, education, work history, family details, language background, finances, and immigration history.
  • Documents and case materials you upload or create, including forms, checklists, evidence, drafts, notes, and status updates.
  • Payment and subscription information processed by payment providers, such as plan, billing status, transaction identifiers, and limited billing details.
  • Usage, device, and analytics information, such as pages viewed, feature activity, IP address, browser type, approximate location, cookies, and similar technologies.
  • Messages and support requests you send to us, including consultation, demo, or advisor marketplace inquiries.

3. How We Use Information

We use information to operate Border AI, run eligibility checks, estimate readiness, generate personalized checklists, organize documents, provide account access, process payments, respond to support requests, improve product quality, secure our services, and comply with legal obligations.

We may use automated systems, including AI tools, to classify information, generate draft guidance, summarize documents, suggest checklist items, and improve the user experience. AI outputs may be incomplete or inaccurate and should be reviewed before you rely on them.

4. How We Share Information

We may share information with service providers that help us host the platform, store files, process payments, provide analytics, send email, secure accounts, and support operations. These providers are authorized to use information only as needed to perform services for us.

When you choose to connect with an advisor or immigration professional, we may share the profile details, documents, notes, and contact information needed to support that request. Advisors and attorneys are independent from Border AI and may have their own terms, privacy practices, and professional duties.

We may also disclose information if required by law, legal process, government request, to enforce our agreements, to protect rights, safety, and security, or in connection with a merger, financing, acquisition, or sale of assets.

We may use cookies, local storage, pixels, and similar technologies to keep you signed in, remember preferences, measure site performance, understand feature usage, and improve Border AI. You can control cookies through your browser settings, but some services may not work correctly if cookies are disabled.

We use administrative, technical, and organizational safeguards designed to protect information. No method of transmission or storage is completely secure, so we cannot guarantee absolute security.

We retain information for as long as needed to provide services, maintain records, resolve disputes, comply with legal obligations, improve safety, and enforce agreements. Retention periods may vary depending on the type of information and your relationship with Border AI.

You may request access, correction, deletion, or export of your personal information, subject to identity verification, legal limits, and records we must keep. You may also unsubscribe from marketing emails by using the link in those messages.

If you ask us to delete information needed to provide a service, we may be unable to continue providing that service. We may keep limited records where required or permitted by law.
